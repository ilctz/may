Martha May is a Croatian indie-pop singer-songwriter currently based in London, UK.
Writing about all sorts of human relationships, emotions, self-love (and hate), her intimate, detailed approach to lyrics gives off her influence from inspiring lyricists such as Lorde, LP, Orla Gartland, and Lana Del Rey, while her ability to make anything sound incredibly catchy comes from listening to popstars like Olivia Rodrigo, Marina, Lady Gaga and Tove Lo.
Martha's love for music and theatre made her move to the United Kingdom at only 18 years old to study Musical Theatre at The University of Chichester. A while later, learning to play guitar in her uni bedroom, Martha discovered she was also a composer and a lyricist. Continuing her music journey, she earned her Master's Degree in Popular Music Practice (songwriting) at the renowned BIMM University in London.
Life in London brought Martha multiple opportunities to showcase her passion for playing to a live audience; she has played her music in some of the most legendary London music venues, such as Spice of Life, Slim Jim's, The Water Rats, The Bedford, and even doing an acoustic set at the opening of London's iconic Battersea Power Station, which was also broadcast by Riverside Radio.
